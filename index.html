About

I’m an Assistant Professor at Boise State University (joining 2025). I research the security and privacy of human–computer interfaces and smart wearables — with emphasis on behavioral biometrics, EEG-based authentication (BrainKey), and adversarial threats to VR/AR systems.

My approach combines signal processing, machine learning, and system-level security to build authentication systems that are robust, privacy-preserving, and deployable in real-world devices. I supervise students, teach courses in cybersecurity and applied machine learning, and collaborate across neuroscience and engineering teams.
