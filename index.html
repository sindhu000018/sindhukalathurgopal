About

I am an Assistant Professor at Boise State University and a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Wyoming (graduating Spring 2025). My research focuses on the offensive and defensive analysis of computing devices and smart wearables, with a specialization in behavioral biometrics, brainwave authentication (EEG), and AI-driven user verification models for VR and AR environments.

My work explores how physiological and behavioral signals—such as EEG activity, eye movements, hand motion, and head dynamics—can be leveraged to build secure, privacy-preserving authentication systems. I also study adversarial attacks, sensor vulnerabilities, and threats to neural and wearable interfaces.
