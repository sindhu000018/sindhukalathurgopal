About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Boise State University. My research lies at the intersection of cybersecurity, machine learning, and human-centered systems, with an emphasis on privacy-preserving, robust, and deployable authentication mechanisms for emerging platforms such as smart wearables and immersive (VR/AR) environments. I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Wyoming, where my doctoral research focused on active authentication, behavioral side-channel analysis, and adversarial robustness in biometric systems. I hold a Master’s (M.Tech.) degree from BITS Pilani (Birla Institute of Technology), India, and a Bachelor’s (B.Tech.) degree from Anna University, completed at Sri Venkateswara College of Engineering (SVCE). My research has been published in top-tier, highly selective, peer-reviewed venues, including the USENIX Security Symposium—a flagship security conference with acceptance rates typically in the 16–20% range—the IEEE International Conference on Automatic Face and Gesture Recognition (FG) (≈30% acceptance rate), and the IEEE International Joint Conference on Biometrics (IJCB) (≈25–35% acceptance rate). I actively contribute to the research community through professional service, having served or currently serving on the Program Committees of IEEE FG, IEEE VR, and IEEE IJCB, and as a member of the Artifact Evaluation Committee for USENIX Security (USENIX Security 2023, 2024, and 2025 Artifacts Tracks: https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity25/call-for-artifacts , https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity24/call-for-artifacts , https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity23/call-for-artifacts ). In addition to my academic background, I bring over nine years of industry experience in enterprise software engineering and system development. I have designed, implemented, and maintained production-scale systems using Java, Spring, Spring Boot, Hibernate, and MVC frameworks, with extensive experience in SQL-based systems (MySQL, Oracle) and CI/CD pipelines. I am deeply committed to graduate and undergraduate mentorship, with a focus on advising students to conduct rigorous, reproducible, and impactful research, while developing the engineering skills necessary to translate ideas into real-world security solutions.
